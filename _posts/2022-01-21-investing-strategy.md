After closely following Mohnish Pabrai and his learnings shared on YouTube, I've decided the best use of my time is to:

1. **Focus on the craft** — get better at coding
2. **Aim for average market returns** via 401k and index-like holdings
3. **Make a few concentrated, long-runway picks** — Nick Sleep style, with competitive advantage and long-term compounding

This way:
- No need to actively manage investments (which consumes significant time)
- More time to improve at the job I enjoy
- Space for a side project that can compound skills AND act as an out-of-the-money call option on an eventual startup
