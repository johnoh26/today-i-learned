- The goal is to double 7 times as fast as possible → 128x return; or 10 times → 1,024x
- At 20–30% annual growth, it takes ~3–4 years to double
- Absent recessions and with consistent growth, it takes ~20–30 years to double 7 times (~40–50 years to double 10 times)
- $100k → ~$10M in 30 years, assuming no recession and persistent growth rate

## Compounder vs. REIT

Thought experiment: both return 20% annually. The compounder reinvests all earnings; the REIT pays them all out.

- Compounder will be 2.5x the value of the REIT in 5 years (assuming constant earnings multiple)
- The REIT sees only ~5% growth from ownership increase, because the returned capital gets reinvested at the same multiple — reducing the effective return from 20% to ~1% (20% ÷ 20x multiple)
- **TL;DR** — long runway + high reinvestment rate is the name of the game

## Other Notes

- ROE may not be as meaningful for tech companies given they don't require traditional capital in product-led growth
- Avoid buybacks unless the expected return is really attractive (price significantly below fair value)
- Dividends are worse than buybacks due to taxes — but better than buybacks if the expected return is unattractive
- If there is nowhere to deploy capital in the business, return it
