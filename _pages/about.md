## Why "Today I Learned"?

Japanese writer Haruki Murakami has a famous daily routine — he consistently writes every morning, no matter how little. This discipline, sustained over decades, made him one of the great writers of our time.

Charlie Munger, Berkshire Hathaway's vice chairman, had a mental model of trying to go to bed a little smarter than when he woke up. This compounding curiosity helped make him one of the great thinkers and investors of the 20th century.

This journal is my attempt to clone both approaches — show up every day, learn something, write it down.

## The Rules

- Write every weekday — no matter how small the entry
- No weekends (healthy habit, not an obsession)
- Review and refine on weekends if the spirit moves

## Topics

The entries span whatever I happened to be learning that day:

- **Tech** — system design, .NET/C#, JavaScript, TypeScript, Angular, databases, networking, architecture, microservices
- **Investing** — compounding, portfolio strategy, mental models
- **Personal** — life reflections, goals, career thoughts
